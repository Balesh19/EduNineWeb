PLACENTA PREVIA BY NARENDRA SIR

Placenta previa is a pregnancy complication where the placenta partially or completely covers the cervix, the opening of the uterus. It is usually diagnosed through an ultrasound, often in the second or third trimester. Common symptoms include bleeding without pain, especially in later stages of pregnancy. Because it can lead to serious hemorrhage, careful monitoring and management are essential. In many cases, bedrest is advised, and a cesarean delivery is planned to avoid complications during labor. Placenta previa poses a risk to both mother and fetus, but with proper obstetric care, healthy outcomes are possible.

HEPATITIES BY SURAJ SIR

Hepatitis is an inflammation of the liver, commonly caused by viral infections such as Hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E. It can also result from excessive alcohol use, certain medications, or autoimmune diseases. Symptoms may include jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, and loss of appetite. Some forms, especially Hepatitis B and C, can become chronic and lead to liver damage, cirrhosis, or even liver cancer. Diagnosis is done through blood tests and liver function tests, and treatment varies depending on the type and severity. Vaccination, safe hygiene practices, and avoiding contaminated food, water, or needles can help prevent certain types of hepatitis.

BODY WEIGHT OF BIRTH BABY BY NARENDRA SIR

The birth weight of a baby is a key sign of its health at birth. A normal weight ranges from 2.5 kg to 4.0 kg. Babies under 2.5 kg are called low birth weight, which can be due to premature birth or maternal health issues. Babies over 4.0 kg may be larger due to diabetes in the mother. Maintaining a healthy pregnancy helps support normal birth weight.
